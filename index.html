Do we live in a fake reality?

Realities, Dec 7, 2021, 12:05 pm

It is not the first time you might have heard of ''social media is harmful to us''
or the counterargument that ''It's helpful if you could give it a chance''

I've always thought of the internet as its own culture, with its own set of celebrities, values, and in-jokes,
as well as sub-sections of the internet/bubbles (eg: clothing, tv shows, beauty-related topics, fashion etc..);
its why a lot of in-jokes don't work in real life, the same way, for example, slang from the UK
about the UK won't work in the USA, and certain things get spread around to broader parts of the
internet while some stay in a smaller niche. Social media's algorithms are making our worlds
so personalized to us that it makes us even more isolated from each other.

I keep thinking about how you can tell a lot from a person if you know
about their interests online. We live in a sort of fake reality online where,
because of our lack of real experiences in certain parts of the real world,
we think that whomever we see or listen to online are just like the
people in the real world, especially now that many of us are
still staying at home during the pandemic.

To a large extent, the replacement of "reality" with "simulacra" and "hyperreality"
is caused by cultural and economic factors, and mediated by accompanying cultural changes.
The commodification of everything that used to have an intrinsic worth in human social life.
Since social media are both a reflection of society, and also constitute a profitable
marketplace of ideas, they are rife with commodified memes mere simulacra of
real-life issues and concerns.

I'm of course no better than a person that does consume media, may I also say,
I've also fallen victim to this manufactured-fake world we live in.
I won't claim that media is bad nor will
I claim that it is destructive.
